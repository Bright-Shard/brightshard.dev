What's a DigiSpark?

DigiSparks are little microcontrollers - basically, tiny boards you can connect lights and buttons to, and then program them to do cool things with those lights and buttons.
The DigiSpark, however, also has USB access. Each board is extremely cheap (Sometimes just $1 each!), and can tell the computer it's connected to that it's a keyboard, not a DigiSpark. Using this, DigiSparks can be used to perform HID attacks.

HID Attacks

HID (Human Interface Device) attacks exploit USB ports to hack computers. Usually, this means telling the computer that whatever device you're plugging in is a keyboard, and then sending keystrokes to open a terminal and pull some funny pranks. The DigiSpark is extremely small and can perform HID attacks, making it a lot of fun to program!
